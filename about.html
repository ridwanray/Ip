History of Ipapo Okun

Ipapo is a town in Itesiwaju Local Government, whose headquarter is situated at Otu. It is situated at longitude 3.5 E and latitude 8.13 N. It has a very large fertile landmark that shares boundaries with Iseyin in Iseyin Local Government at the South and Tede town in Atisbo Local Government at the North, ILERO on Iwajowa Local Government. Ipapo has a population of 124,000 people (National Population Census,2016).

The Omo Sanda festival in Ipapo, symbolizes the commencement of the new yam festival calendar before Eleyinpo will start to eat yam in the year. It is discourage for an Eleyinpo to eat yam in any year without performing the yam festival. On every year the date of the festival must be transmitted to Alaafin of Oyo because since time memorial His Royal majesty, the Alaafin Oba Lamidi Olayiwola Adeyemi III Alaafin will be the one that will provide the ritual ram for Eleyinpo with six representative that must spend seven days in Ipapo before returning to Oyo. It is still practice till today, 21st century in Ipapo.

The 7th Eleyinpo of Ipapo, Oba Fatumo settled Oke-amu separately on a landspace where they are till today.

The Nigeria Police Force commences operation in Ipapo in 1983 during the reign of Oba Eleyinpo Amusat Abioye I. The first officer to manned the station was an Ijesha sergeant a.k.a baba ofero(Baba no like lie). The New Police Station Structure was built with community efforts anchored by Ipapo Descendant Union (IDU) under the leadership of Alh Jimoh Opoola Adebayo (November 2020)

OUR PARTICIPATION AT THE ITESIWAJU LOCAL GOVERNMENT,OTU
Prior to the Creation of Itesiwaju Local Government,Ipapo was ward 12 under the Iseyin Local Government council that contained ipapo and oke-amu as a single voting Ward.The last representative that we had at counsellor level were All Abdul Azeez Ako from NRC while Alh Ogunleti was from Social Democratic Party (SDP) in 1995-1997. They were in office when the Itesieaju Local Government was created
The Itesiwaju Local Government was carved out of Old Iseyin Local Government in 1996 by the military regime of Late General Sanni Abacha. The first Sole administrator to chaired the council was our father, elder J.O Ogunyale from Arero compound of Ipapo (December 1996- March 1997). The Local Government Area comprises of seven towns, namely: Ipapo, Oke+amu, Okaka, Otu, Komu, Gbojaiye, and Bab-ode Mr Aikiola Tijani was elected as the Vice-chairman of the council between (2003-2006). Mr Azeez Idowu Olaniyi was elected as the Chairman to head the administration of the council between (2007-2010). Ipapo has two electoral wards which are ward 3 and watd 10. The current representatives of the town at the council level are Hon.Salau Nurudeen Olaniyi representing ward 3 while Hon. Ojo Taiwo Adesola represent ward 10
